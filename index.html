Quick & Easy Setup

Clickr consists of two components. One is the iOS app. The other is a Mac app.

The single thing you have to do to start watching videos, is to install an app on your Mac.

Nothing else is required. We worked extremely hard to make the two parts of Clickr be as automatic as possible. If they are on the same network, they’ll make everything possible to connect and do their job.

The Mac component is housed inside your iOS device, in the iOS Clickr app. You'll always have it with you no matter where you take your phone. Going to your friend's house? Just activate the setup server in Clickr on your phone and you can start using another Mac.

Clear & Simple Design

You want to watch videos. It's that simple. Your phone or your computer - it doesn't matter. You don't have to be nailed in front of one and you don't want to stare at the small screen of the other. We want to remove all constraints for you. Be free watch videos the way you want.

Select a bunch of items and Clickr will play them from first to last. On your phone or your computer.

It's really hard to explaint with words how clear and simple the design is. We try our hardest to remove any configuration steps. The two components of Clickr comunicate over the local network, but your computer would just seem like another screen on your phone.

Technical Details

Simple concept this whole thing. Very difficult to design the machinery that makes it work.

Clickr is for now only compatible with Mac. We'll fix that so you can use it with Windows and Linux.

The two components of Clickr talk to each other over a local IPv4 network, they use both TCP and UDP with a custom network protocol. If they are not both connected to the same router, on the same subnet they can't find eachother. We are now testing ways to make them find eachother over the greater internet. We'll remove that constraint too. Soon.
